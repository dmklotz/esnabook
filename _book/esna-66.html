The Temple of Esna: a Continual Translation

Esna 66

  • Location: Facade, intercolumnar bandeau
  • Date: Unknown, probably Claudius
  • Hieroglyphic Text
  • Bibliography: None
mj m ḥtp
ẖnm=k pr-wr m pr-m-qd
ʿq=k ḥw.t-nṯr m nḏm-jb
   wʿb.tw nn ḏw nb ḫr=k

mḥn=j ḥr tp=k
    mj nb-ḏr
mnḥ.n=j tpy.w nw ḫfty.w=k
   m rn=j pfy n Mnḥy.t

Come in peace!6
Unite with the Per-wer in extasy,
enter the temple in joy,
   being pure, without evil.

I am coiled (mḥn) upon your head,
    like (I would for) the All Lord,
I have cut off (mnḥ) the heads of your enemies,
   in my name of Menhyt (Mnḥy.t).

  1. This text is properly speaking a caption above Esna 67. The goddess Menhyt addresses the king while he is being purified by Harsiese and Thoth.↩
